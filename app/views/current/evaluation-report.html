{# Executive Summary #}

Executive summary

Leeds Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust conducted a 6-month clinical trial evaluating the SmartPump 300 Infusion System in the Critical Care Unit at St James's University Hospital. The trial compared the SmartPump 300 against our existing infusion pump system with a focus on medication administration safety, staff satisfaction, and operational efficiency.

The evaluation demonstrated a 45% reduction in medication administration errors and high levels of staff satisfaction. Integration with our Epic electronic health record system was seamless. Based on these positive outcomes, the Trust approved procurement and trust-wide rollout in August 2023.

Information:

Key outcome: 45% reduction in medication administration errors compared to previous system

{# Evaluation Approach #}

Evaluation approach

Methodology

We conducted a prospective, controlled clinical trial over 6 months. Twenty-four SmartPump 300 devices were deployed in the Critical Care Unit alongside our existing infusion pump fleet, allowing for direct comparison of outcomes.

Evaluation criteria

  • Clinical safety: Medication administration error rates, adverse drug events, alarm management
  • Staff experience: User satisfaction, training time requirements, workflow integration
  • Technical performance: Device reliability, battery life, connectivity
  • System integration: EHR interoperability, drug library management, audit trail functionality
  • Business case: Total cost of ownership, error reduction value, efficiency gains

Data collection

We collected quantitative data on error rates, device uptime, and training hours. Qualitative feedback was gathered through nursing staff surveys (n=58), focus groups (n=3), and pharmacy team interviews (n=4). Clinical outcomes were tracked through our incident reporting system and EHR analytics.

{# Key Findings #}

Key findings

Clinical safety and outcomes

Medication administration errors

45% reduction in medication administration errors compared to previous system during the trial period.

  • Previous system: 22 errors per 10,000 infusions
  • SmartPump 300: 12 errors per 10,000 infusions
  • Most common errors prevented: incorrect rate programming, wrong concentration

Nursing staff confidence

Staff reported significantly improved confidence in medication administration safety.

  • 87% of nursing staff rated the drug library alerts as "very helpful" or "extremely helpful"
  • 92% felt more confident administering high-risk medications
  • Staff particularly valued the clear visual interface and soft limit warnings

System integration

Epic EHR integration

Seamless integration with our Epic Hyperspace system using HL7 FHIR standards.

  • Automatic population of infusion orders from Epic
  • Real-time infusion data flowing back to patient chart
  • No manual transcription required
  • Pharmacy team reported improved oversight and audit capabilities

Staff training and adoption

Training efficiency

Training time was significantly shorter than anticipated.

  • Budgeted: 4 hours per staff member
  • Actual average: 2 hours per staff member
  • 50% reduction in training time saved approximately £8,400 in staff costs
  • Staff found the touchscreen interface intuitive and similar to consumer devices

Technical performance

Device uptime: 99.7% (exceeding manufacturer specification of 99.5%)
Wi-Fi connectivity: Stable throughout trial period after initial network configuration
Battery performance: Average 6.5 hours (manufacturer spec: 7 hours)

{# Challenges and Concerns #}

Challenges and concerns

Important: Challenges identified during evaluation

Alert fatigue (initial)

During the first 2 weeks of deployment, nursing staff reported alert fatigue due to frequent soft limit warnings. This was resolved by:

  • Refining drug library soft limits in collaboration with pharmacy
  • Providing additional training on alert interpretation
  • Customizing alerting thresholds for ICU patient population

Outcome: Alert acceptance rate improved from 68% to 94% after adjustments.

Battery life

Battery life was slightly shorter than manufacturer specification in high-use scenarios (average 6.5 hours vs specified 7 hours).

  • Particularly noticeable during busy shifts with continuous use
  • Required more frequent charging than anticipated
  • Supplier acknowledged the issue and provided additional battery packs

Mitigation: Increased charging station availability and implemented battery rotation protocol.

Value and benefits

Our business case demonstrated significant value through:

  • Error reduction: Estimated £180,000 annual savings from avoided adverse drug events and associated care costs
  • Efficiency gains: 15 minutes saved per patient per day = approximately £95,000 annual staff time savings
  • Reduced documentation burden: Automatic EHR documentation saves approximately 8 minutes per infusion
  • Audit and compliance: Comprehensive audit trail reduces investigation time for incidents
Information:

Return on investment: Estimated 24-month payback period based on error reduction and efficiency gains

{# Decision Factors #}

Decision factors

Our procurement decision was based on four key factors:

1. Clinical safety

The 45% reduction in medication errors was the most significant factor. Patient safety improvements aligned with our Quality Improvement Programme objectives and CQC action plan requirements.

2. EHR integration capability

Seamless Epic integration was essential. Previous systems required manual data entry and transcription, creating additional error risks and staff burden. The SmartPump 300's HL7 FHIR compatibility met our interoperability requirements.

3. Staff training requirements

The intuitive interface and shorter-than-expected training time reduced implementation barriers. High staff satisfaction scores indicated strong user acceptance, which was critical for trust-wide adoption.

4. Total cost of ownership

While acquisition costs were higher than some alternatives, the business case demonstrated strong value through error reduction and efficiency gains. No proprietary consumables requirement kept ongoing costs manageable.

{# Implementation Lessons #}

Implementation lessons learned

What worked well

  • Early pharmacy engagement – involving pharmacy in drug library configuration from day one prevented many potential alert fatigue issues
  • Phased training approach – training "super users" first who then supported colleagues reduced training team burden
  • Supplier support during go-live – having supplier engineers on-site for the first week resolved teething issues quickly
  • Weekly review meetings – first 6 weeks of weekly MDT meetings allowed rapid problem-solving and adjustment

What we'd do differently

  • More charging stations upfront – we underestimated charging needs and had to order additional stations mid-trial
  • Earlier Wi-Fi testing – network connectivity issues in week 1 could have been identified during pre-deployment testing
  • Longer pilot period consideration – some staff felt 6 months was tight for capturing seasonal variation and edge cases
{# Recommendations #}

Recommendations for other trusts

Based on our experience, we would recommend the SmartPump 300 to other trusts, particularly those:

  • Using Epic or other HL7 FHIR-compliant EHR systems
  • Looking to reduce medication administration errors
  • Seeking to improve infusion pump fleet standardization
  • Requiring strong audit trail and compliance capabilities

Important considerations for evaluation:

  • Plan for 2-3 weeks of drug library optimization with your pharmacy team
  • Ensure adequate Wi-Fi coverage and bandwidth in deployment areas
  • Budget for additional charging infrastructure beyond manufacturer recommendations
  • Allow sufficient time for staff training, even though it's quicker than traditional pumps
  • Establish clear escalation paths for technical issues during go-live
{# Conclusion #}

Conclusion

The SmartPump 300 Infusion System met or exceeded expectations across all evaluation criteria. The 45% reduction in medication administration errors, combined with high staff satisfaction and seamless EHR integration, provided clear evidence of clinical value.

While we encountered minor challenges with alert optimization and battery management, these were successfully resolved through collaboration with the supplier and our clinical teams. The business case demonstrated strong return on investment through error reduction and efficiency gains.

Based on the trial outcomes, Leeds Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust approved procurement and began trust-wide rollout in August 2023. We are now deploying SmartPump 300 devices across all inpatient wards and theatres, with full implementation expected by March 2024.
